Logos

λóγος — The word as reason; the principle of cosmic order.

GREEK PHILOSOPHY COGNITION
[01] PROTO-INDO-EUROPEAN

*leǵ-

Meaning: "to collect, gather," with derivatives meaning "to speak" (on the notion of "to pick out words").

CONNECTION STRONG

Lex — (Latin) Law, that which is collected/bound.

[02] ARCHAIC GREECE

Légein

To speak, to tell, to recount. The shift from physical gathering to the gathering of thoughts into speech.

ASSOCIATED TEXTS:
  • Homer, The Iliad (Book IX)
  • Hesiod, Theogony
[03] HERACLITEAN SHIFT

Logos

Universal reason. The governing principle that makes the world intelligible. "All things come to be in accordance with this Logos."

auto_awesome_motion Mapped to: Dialectics
[04] MODERN SYNTHESIS

Logic

The formalization of Logos into a tool of measurement and valid inference. Separation of 'word' and 'truth'.

MARGIN NOTE: "Observe the transmutation from cosmic principle to mechanical procedure."
